04) SHIPS, FLEETS, AND MOVEMENT
--------------------------------

THE ROSTER LOGIC
----------------
Every culture fields the same broad battlefield jobs:
- `Intelligence`: spy and recon hulls
- `Drone` / `Skirmisher` / `Corvette`: fast light pressure and tracking support
- `Starfighter` / `Gunship` / `Cruiser` / `Destroyer` / `Capital`: main combat
  line with different target preferences
- `Carrier`: weak direct statline, but buffs every non-carrier in the fleet
- `Siege`: building-breaker
- `Colonization`: expansion hull
- `Flagship`: custom late-game apex hull

SHIP COST: THE EXACT RULE
-------------------------
Each hull has a base cost number in the live roster data.
The actual per-resource build cost is derived from that number.

Base weight rule:
- Vulkron  = `baseCost * 1.25`
- Aurelite = `baseCost * 0.95`
- Deuterium = `baseCost * 0.95`
- Astra = `baseCost * 0.85`

Then each culture gets a `50%` discount on one resource:
- Astraean -> Aurelite
- Varkon -> Vulkron
- Veil -> Deuterium

WORKED EXAMPLE: REAL SHIP COST
------------------------------
Take the Astraean `Comet Rapier`, which has a base cost of `84`.

Step 1: apply universal weights

`Vulkron  = 84 * 1.25 = 105.0`
`Aurelite = 84 * 0.95 = 79.8`
`Deuterium = 84 * 0.95 = 79.8`
`Astra = 84 * 0.85 = 71.4`

Step 2: apply the Astraean discount on Aurelite

`Aurelite = 79.8 * 0.5 = 39.9`

Final Astraean Comet Rapier cost:
- `105.0 Vulkron`
- `39.9 Aurelite`
- `79.8 Deuterium`
- `71.4 Astra`

TRAINING TIME: THE EXACT RULE
-----------------------------
For ships, the game uses:

`actualPerUnitTime = listedPerUnitTime / ShipBuildSpeedMultiplier`

Then unit-specific SPU training bonuses apply:

`actualPerUnitTime = actualPerUnitTime / (1 + unitTrainingBonus)`

The result cannot go below `1 second` per unit.

Ship build speed comes from:
- the relevant shipyard for that hull family
- some Eldritch upgrades
- SPUs
- Astraean penalty

Important culture rule:
- Astraean empires multiply ship build speed by `0.70`
- that means Astraean ship queues are slower unless boosted elsewhere

TRAINING LANES
--------------
Training is split by hull family, not by one global queue.

Each completed production building adds one lane for its own family:
- each `Light Shipyard` copy adds one light/intelligence lane
- each `Heavy Shipyard` copy adds one heavy/cargo/colonization lane
- each `Siege Foundry` copy adds one bomber/siege lane

That means a base can naturally train in parallel across light, heavy, and
siege families once those buildings exist.

If a base has `Eldritch Conduit` plus `Riftwalk Logistics`, the lane count for
each unlocked hull family is doubled.

That upgrade is a major tempo spike because one specialized base can keep
multiple answers building at once inside the same hull family.

FLEET MOVEMENT: THE REAL FORMULA
--------------------------------
The fleet uses the slowest ship in the group after modifiers.

Exact rule:

`travelHours = distance / (slowestShipSpeed * baseTravelMultiplier * globalTravelMultiplier)`

Where:
- `slowestShipSpeed` already includes carrier aura and unit speed SPUs
- `baseTravelMultiplier` comes from the relevant shipyard, SPUs, and some Eldritch upgrades
- `globalTravelMultiplier` is `3.0x` in the standard campaign
- minimum fleet travel time is `0.01h`

WORKED EXAMPLE: FLEET MOVEMENT
------------------------------
Suppose a fleet must cross distance `30`.

The slowest ship after all ship-level modifiers is speed `5.0`.
The origin has `Heavy Shipyard 10`, so:

`baseTravelMultiplier = 1.0 + (0.10 * 10) = 2.0`

No other launch bonuses apply, and the campaign uses the standard global
travel multiplier:

`globalTravelMultiplier = 3.0`

Now calculate:

`travelHours = 30 / (5.0 * 2.0 * 3.0)`
`travelHours = 30 / 30`
`travelHours = 1.0`

That fleet arrives in exactly `1 hour`.

CARRIER AURA
------------
Carriers buff every non-carrier in the fleet.

Exact rule:

`carrierBuff = 1.0 + (carrierCount * 0.0001)`

Then every non-carrier stat contribution that uses `BuffForUnit(...)` is
multiplied by that value.

Important:
- carriers do not buff themselves
- the aura is small per carrier, but it affects attack, defense, speed, cargo,
  and siege calculations wherever the unit stat is sampled

CARGO CAPACITY
--------------
Fleet cargo capacity is:

`sum(shipCount * shipCargo * carrierBuffIfNonCarrier) * fleetCargoMultiplier`

`fleetCargoMultiplier` can come from:
- Orbital Exchange level at launch base
- Veil `Rift Cargo Manifolds`
- SPU fleet cargo bonuses

Cargo matters for:
- raids
- manual shipments
- marketplace trades

Important:
- `Orbital Exchange` improves launched-fleet cargo directly
- the same building also speeds up logistics travel for transfers, trade
  routes, and marketplace fulfillment

MISSION TYPES
-------------

ATTACK
- Full battle.
- One side is forced to break completely.
- Best for conquest, decisive punishes, and base destruction through siege.

RAID
- Softer casualty rules.
- Used to steal resources without always committing to a wipe battle.
- Hidden resources, cargo limits, and raid multipliers matter more than raw
  brute force alone.

REINFORCE
- Move ships into another garrison.
- Best used to harden exposed bases before a real war starts.

COLONIZE
- Requires at least one `Colonization` hull.
- If the destination is empty and legal, a colony is founded and one colony hull
  is consumed.
- If the destination is hostile and occupied, the colonization fleet fights.

SPY
- Requires `Intelligence` ships only.
- Used to reveal real base state before you launch a larger fleet.

TRADE SHIPMENTS AND MARKET TRAVEL
---------------------------------
Manual shipments and accepted marketplace trades use a dedicated logistics speed:

`tradeSpeed = 5.0 * baseTravelMultiplier * globalTravelMultiplier * shipmentSpeedMultiplier`

Where:
- `baseTravelMultiplier` comes from the relevant shipyard/travel bonuses
- `shipmentSpeedMultiplier` includes `Orbital Exchange` and any other logistics
  shipment-speed bonuses

Then:

`travelHours = distance / max(0.1, tradeSpeed)`

This means shipping speed is not tied to the specific cargo hull's listed ship
speed. It is tied to the logistics rule above plus the source base's travel
multipliers.

FLEET-BUILDING ADVICE
---------------------
1. Do not build only heavy ships.
   You still need tracking, speed, and a light screen.

2. Do not raid with fleets that cannot carry meaningful loot.
   Winning a battle and carrying home almost nothing is a tempo trap.

3. Siege ships belong in attack fleets, not every fleet.
   If the goal is loot or scouting pressure, spare yourself the speed penalty.

4. Colonization hulls are strategic cargo.
   Do not float them around casually on exposed lanes.

5. Intelligence is not optional.
   Spy hulls save more ships than they cost.

CULTURE IDENTITY AT A GLANCE
----------------------------

ASTRAEAN
- slower builders
- strong utility, cargo, tracking, and flexible line pieces
- good for measured expansions and efficient mixed fleets

VARKON
- attack and raid fleets gain a huge `3.0x` attack multiplier
- best culture for decisive committed violence
- strongest when fights happen on purpose, not by accident

VEIL
- fastest strategic toolkit
- best at tempo tricks, evasion, raid shaping, shard economy, and launch-base
  specialization

WHEN TO ADD A FLAGSHIP
----------------------
Add a flagship when:
- the research base is safe
- the rest of your empire already works
- you know what stat profile your current match actually needs

Do not start flagship plans so early that they delay shipyard tempo, Spire progress,
or the first serious fleet.
