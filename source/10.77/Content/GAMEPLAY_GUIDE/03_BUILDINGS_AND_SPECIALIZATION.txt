03) BUILDINGS AND SPECIALIZATION
--------------------------------

GENERAL BUILDING RULES
----------------------
Most buildings scale to level 20.

Important exceptions:
- Dyson Sphere: max level 200
- Flagship Research: max level 5
- Drill and Refiner: mining-outpost structures, not normal colony builds

GENERAL BUILD-TIME RULE
-----------------------
Building costs scale by structure type and level, but the timing rule is simple:

`actualBuildTime = listedBuildTime / BuildSpeedMultiplier`

Your base's `BuildSpeedMultiplier` starts at `1.0` and then grows from:
- Central Spire
- Flagship Research
- some Eldritch upgrades
- some SPU bonuses

Important culture rule:
- Astraean empires apply a 0.60x multiplier to normal building speed
- that means Astraean colonies build structures more slowly unless other bonuses
  make up for it

WORKED EXAMPLE: BUILD SPEED
---------------------------
Suppose a base has:
- Central Spire 8
- no other build-speed effects

Then:

`BuildSpeedMultiplier = 1.0 + (0.10 * 8) = 1.8`

If a structure has a listed build time of `110` seconds:

`actual time = 110 / 1.8 = 61.1 seconds`

If the same base also has Flagship Research 5, add another:

`0.03 * 5 = 0.15`

Now:

`BuildSpeedMultiplier = 1.95`

And:

`110 / 1.95 = 56.4 seconds`

THE MOST IMPORTANT BUILDINGS
----------------------------

1. CENTRAL SPIRE
- Exact effect: +10% building speed per level
- Strategic use: the backbone of expansion and late-game victory
- Critical milestones:
  - L5/L10/L15: each level unlocks one generic outpost slot from that base
  - L20: colony founding unlocks from that base
  - L21-L25: each level adds another generic outpost slot
  - each outpost slot can be spent on a strategic outpost, asteroid mining
    outpost, or rare-metal mining outpost if the target tile is valid
  - normal Central Spires no longer directly satisfy victory; `(0,0)` uses a
    culture-specific Wormhole instead

2. SHIPYARD FAMILIES
- The legacy `Dockyards` building is retired. Live production uses three
  separate shipyard families.
- `Light Shipyard`: +10% light/intelligence build and travel speed per level,
  plus +2% light-hull stats per level
- `Heavy Shipyard`: +10% heavy/cargo/colonization build and travel speed per
  level, plus +2% heavy-hull stats per level
- `Siege Foundry`: +10% bomber/siege build and travel speed per level, plus
  +2% siege-hull stats per level
- Unlock thresholds: bombers require `Siege Foundry 5`; siege hulls require
  `Siege Foundry 15`
- Strategic use: specialize launch bases around the hull families they actually
  produce and send

3. ION SHIELD
- Exact effect depends on the owner's culture:
  - Astraean: +7.5% defense multiplier and +75 flat base defense per level
    (2.5x shield strength and +1,500 flat defense at L20)
  - Varkon: +1% defense multiplier and +10 flat base defense per level
    (1.2x shield strength and +200 flat defense at L20)
  - Veil: +5% defense multiplier and +50 flat base defense per level
    (2.0x shield strength and +1,000 flat defense at L20)
- Strategic use: turns a colony from "raided if seen" into a position that
  demands real commitment to break
- Hidden catch: shield integrity matters; a damaged shield is much weaker

4. OBJECTIVE STRUCTURES
- Normal colonies no longer build vision infrastructure.
- `(0,0)` can build only its culture-specific Wormhole, Silo, Solar Cell, and
  Ion Shield.
- Keystone colonies can build only Keystone Vision, Silo, Solar Cell, and Ion
  Shield.
- Wormhole and Keystone Vision levels are gated by the lower of Silo and Solar
  Cell on the same special colony.
- Strategic use: turns defended objective control into victory progress
- Common mistake: funding the structure before the site can be held

5. SILO AND SOLAR CELL
- Strategic use: they are tempo buildings, not dead weight
- Without them you hard-cap income and waste production
- Silo: Vulkron, Aurelite, Deuterium
- Solar Cell: Astra

6. ORBITAL EXCHANGE
- Exact effect: `+1%` cargo capacity per level for launched fleets
- Exact effect: `+1%` shipment speed per level for trade routes, manual
  transfers, and marketplace fulfillment
- Strategic use: stronger raids, stronger logistics, stronger market shipping
- Best on bases that launch traders, raiders, or long-haul support fleets

RESOURCE BUILDINGS
------------------
Matching resource buildings add directly to the corresponding multiplier:
- Extractor -> Vulkron
- Synthesiser -> Aurelite
- Combinator -> Deuterium
- Solar Array -> Astra
- Dyson Sphere -> Astra, but as a late-game sink with a far higher cap

In the standard campaign, each normal resource-building level adds +10% to the
matching resource output. Custom presets can change that bonus, but the
building rule is always "campaign normal bonus per level."

Dyson Sphere note:
- it follows the same Astra multiplier idea
- it exists to keep late-game Astra scaling relevant
- after level 20 its cost keeps climbing aggressively

DEFENSIVE AND ECONOMIC UTILITY BUILDINGS
----------------------------------------

NANO STORAGE
- Exact effect:

  `hiddenFraction = min(0.80, 0.04 * level)`

- Best use: backline stockpiles, raid-prone frontiers, and objective support
  economies

DRONE FACILITY
- Exact effect:

  `upkeepReduction = min(0.80, 0.04 * level)`

- Best use: big garrisons, training hubs, and Astra-starved war economies

FLAGSHIP RESEARCH
- Max level 5
- Exact effect: +15% research speed per level
- Exact effect: +3% build speed per level
- Best use: a safe infrastructure base that will stay alive long enough to
  complete a flagship program

Flagship research point sources:
- Light Attack: `0.1` per light hull destroyed while attacking
- Heavy Attack: `0.1` per heavy hull destroyed while attacking
- Light Defense: `0.1` per light hull destroyed while defending
- Heavy Defense: `0.1` per heavy hull destroyed while defending
- Speed: `0.0001` per resource raided
- Cargo: `0.01` per resource moved in accepted exchange offers
- Crit: `0.1` per completed contract
- Evasion: `0.1` per defended incoming spy mission
- Tracking: `0.1` per successful outgoing spy mission
- Siege: `1.0` per building level destroyed

CULTURE-SPECIFIC INFRASTRUCTURE
-------------------------------

ASTRAEAN: OVERCLOCKER
- In the standard campaign, each level adds +5% to all four resource outputs
- In custom campaigns, it always uses half of that save's normal
  resource-building bonus
- Best use: balanced economy bases and long-ramp macro builds

VARKON: CONSCRIPTION SERVICE
- Active ability, not a passive economy building
- Activation cost:

  `200 * buildingLevel` of each core resource

- Active window:

  scales linearly from `15m` at L1 to `4h` at L20

- Cooldown:

  after the active window ends, scales linearly from `24h` at L1 to `16h` at L20

- Effect:

  `+10% * buildingLevel` attack power to the owner while active

Worked example:
- `Conscription Service 7`
- activation cost = `1,400` Vulkron + `1,400` Aurelite + `1,400` Deuterium +
  `1,400` Astra
- active window = about `1h 26m`
- cooldown after the window = about `21h 28m`
- effect = `+70%` attack power during that window

VEIL: ELDRITCH CONDUIT
- unlocks a deep shard-powered upgrade tree
- exact upgrade list appears later in `Veil, Mining Outposts, and SPUs`
- best use: a carefully chosen launch base, fortress base, or raid hub

VEIL: TRANSMUTING VOID
- converts any mix of input resources into one chosen output resource at `1:1`
  total volume
- duration starts at 6h at level 1 and falls to 15m at level 20
- best use: fixing short-term bottlenecks, not replacing real production

HOW TO SPECIALIZE BASES
-----------------------
Trying to do everything everywhere usually creates slow, fragile empires.

Strong specializations:

1. Economic engine
- matching resource building
- storage
- Overclocker or economy SPUs if available
- usually lower garrison unless exposed

2. Military forge
- shipyards
- Astra support
- Drone Facility if garrison is large
- enough storage to keep training uninterrupted

3. Objective node
- Wormhole or Keystone Vision
- gated storage
- strong Astra economy
- real defense, because a lost objective contributes nothing

4. Fortress
- Ion Shield
- Nano Storage
- enough shipyard tempo or nearby support that reinforcements arrive in time
- use on choke points, core sites, or high-value production worlds

5. Launch hub
- shipyards
- Orbital Exchange
- culture-specific launch bonuses
- best place for raids, market shipments, and deep-strike fleets

COMMON BUILDING MISTAKES
------------------------
1. Objective funding before economy.
   If Wormhole or Keystone upgrades starve the rest of your empire, you slowed
   yourself down.

2. No storage on a rich tile.
   A capped base is wasting output.

3. Frontline colony without shipyard support.
   If help cannot arrive quickly, the defense is weaker than it looks.

4. Too many half-built roles.
   One real military base and one real economy base are usually better than
   four unfinished hybrids.
