09) MARKETS, ORGANIZATIONS, AND DIPLOMACY
-----------------------------------------

ORBITAL EXCHANGE AND THE RESOURCE MARKET
----------------------------------------
Marketplace offers are strict `1-for-1` listings:
- exactly one offered resource type
- exactly one wanted resource type

Posting an offer requires:
- enough stock to cover the offered goods
- enough cargo ships to cover that shipment

Those cargo ships are reserved while the offer is live.

Accepting an offer requires:
- enough stock to pay the wanted amount
- enough cargo capacity to haul the payment

MARKET SHIPMENTS
----------------
Accepted trades create two travel legs:
- seller goods travel to buyer
- buyer goods travel to seller

Travel uses the logistics rule from section `04`.

Practical market lesson:
- Orbital Exchange on a launch base is a real trade advantage
- high travel speed shortens capital lock-up
- weak cargo means attractive offers may still be impossible to execute

ORGANIZATIONS: WHAT THEY ARE
----------------------------
Organizations are player-facing financial institutions that can act as:
- contract boards
- lenders
- holding vehicles

A single organization can lean toward one role or become a hybrid.

FOUNDING RULES
--------------
- ticker must be exactly `3` letters
- minimum seed is `1 Noctmark`
- starting capital structure:
  - authorized shares = `100`
  - founder-issued shares = `100`
  - public float = `0`
- initial share price:

  `max(1, round(seedNoctmarks / 100))`

So a `500` Noctmark seed implies a starting share price near `5`.

CONTRACTS
---------
Contracts pay in `Noctmarks`.

Current contract types:
- destroy a base
- destroy building levels
- destroy ship Astra value
- raid resources total
- raid one resource type
- destroy a mining outpost
- break Ion Shield integrity
- raid Astra total

How to use them:
- take contracts that match what your fleet already wants to do
- post contracts that make rivals bleed in ways your own empire benefits from

The organization UI already exposes:
- recommended payout
- recent sold average
- offer quality
- risk band
- summary text

That is the best player-facing way to judge whether a contract is cheap,
credible, or bait.

SHARES AND THE EQUITY MARKET
----------------------------
Organizations support:
- sell orders
- bid orders
- completed trades
- price history

Useful readings on the owner page:
- `Last trade`
- `Fair value`
- `Market cap`
- `Enterprise value`
- float
- ownership concentration
- dividend policy

Player lesson:
- `Last trade` tells you what just cleared
- `Fair value` tells you what the business looks like underneath the tape
- the gap between them tells you whether the market is hot, weak, or starved

LOANS
-----
Organizations can lend and borrow in `Noctmarks`.

Daily interest rule:

`dailyInterest = ceil(outstandingPrincipal * dailyRatePercent / 100)`

WORKED EXAMPLE: LOAN INTEREST
-----------------------------
Suppose a board borrows `12,000 Noctmarks` at `1.8%` daily.

`dailyInterest = ceil(12000 * 1.8 / 100)`
`dailyInterest = ceil(216)`
`dailyInterest = 216`

That borrower owes `216 Noctmarks` every day until principal is repaid or the
loan matures.

DEFAULTS
--------
If a borrower cannot cover daily interest, the loan can default.
When that happens:
- the lender seizes whatever treasury it can
- the unpaid remainder is recorded as unrecovered
- the credit relationship worsens

Loan lesson:
- borrowed cash should fund a plan that earns before the interest clock wins

TRIBUTE, TRUCES, AND DIPLOMATIC PRESSURE
----------------------------------------
Players can use diplomacy to send:
- truce offers
- Noctmark tribute
- resource tribute

Tribute matters because it can:
- move stock to an ally at the right time
- improve relations
- buy short-term stability inside a faction network

Important detail:
- `Noctmarks` go to the receiving leader's personal account
- resource tribute goes to a receiving base

ATTACKS AND PENALTIES
---------------------
Raids and attacks create diplomatic friction.

Veil `Phantom Treaty` is powerful because it cuts those attack and raid
penalties by `80%` for fleets launched from that base.

TRANSMISSIONS
-------------
The Transmissions beacon gives you short interactive events with real gameplay
consequences.

Exact flow:
- start a search
- wait `10` to `30` seconds
- pick one of three responses
- after choosing, the beacon cools down for `10 minutes`

Transmission outcomes can change:
- resources
- faction relations
- ships
- building levels

Because transmission effects hit a real target base, they are strongest when
used on the base that can convert the outcome immediately.

USEFUL ECONOMIC USES OF THESE SYSTEMS
-------------------------------------
1. Use the market to convert local surpluses into missing war inputs.

2. Use organizations when your empire is rich enough that capital structure and
   credit actually matter.

3. Use contracts to pay for pressure you wanted on the map anyway.

4. Use transmissions opportunistically, not habitually. Their value depends on
   current context.

5. Use tribute when a shared defensive line matters more than perfect selfish
   efficiency.

COMMON MISUSES
--------------
1. Posting contracts you cannot really fund.

2. Selling shares too cheaply just because you want instant cash.

3. Borrowing for vanity expansion instead of a clear timing window.

4. Treating diplomacy as flavor text while your frontier burns.
