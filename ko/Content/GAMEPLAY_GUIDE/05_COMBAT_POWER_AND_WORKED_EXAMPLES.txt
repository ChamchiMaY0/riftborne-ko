05) COMBAT POWER AND WORKED EXAMPLES
------------------------------------

THIS SECTION MATTERS MORE THAN ANY OTHER
----------------------------------------
Combat decides:
- whether your raid actually pays
- whether your shield line really holds
- whether siege can break a base this trip
- whether your "safe" economy is actually safe

The game does not use one flat attack stat.
It builds power from target mix, defender mix, crit, tracking, evasion,
carriers, attack buffs, attack multipliers, and base defense.

STEP 1: LIGHT SHARE AND HEAVY SHARE
-----------------------------------
Before power is computed, each side is split into a light fraction and a heavy
fraction based on the actual hulls present.

For a side with:
- `lightCount`
- `heavyCount`

The shares are:

`lightShare = lightCount / (lightCount + heavyCount)`
`heavyShare = 1 - lightShare`

Why this matters:
- anti-light ships gain value against light-heavy fleets
- anti-heavy ships gain value against capital-heavy fleets
- mixed fleets are harder to counter cleanly

STEP 2: CARRIER AURA
--------------------
Carriers buff every non-carrier in their own fleet.

Exact rule:

`carrierBuff = 1.0 + (carrierCount * 0.001)`

This value is applied to non-carrier ship stats in battle calculations.
In plain terms, each carrier adds `+0.1%` to non-carrier ship stats.

STEP 3: WEIGHTED ATTACK FOR EACH UNIT
-------------------------------------
Every ship converts its split attack into one weighted value against the
defender's current mix:

`weightedAttack = (AttackVsLight * defenderLightShare +`
`                  AttackVsHeavy * defenderHeavyShare) * carrierBuffIfNonCarrier`

If an SPU gives that unit class attack bonus, the game multiplies again:

`weightedAttack = weightedAttack * (1 + unitAttackBonus)`

STEP 4: WEIGHTED DEFENSE FOR EACH UNIT
-------------------------------------
Every ship also converts its split defense into one weighted value against the
attacker's mix:

`weightedDefense = (DefenseVsLight * attackerLightShare +`
`                   DefenseVsHeavy * attackerHeavyShare) * carrierBuffIfNonCarrier`

If an SPU gives that unit class defense bonus, the game multiplies again:

`weightedDefense = weightedDefense * (1 + unitDefenseBonus)`

STEP 5: ATTACKER POWER
----------------------
First the game sums weighted attack:

`attackerAttackBase = sum(shipCount * weightedAttack)`

If the owner has an active attack buff, it applies here.
For example, an active `Conscription Service 7` gives `+70%` attack:

`attackerAttackBase = attackerAttackBase * 1.70`

Then the game calculates average crit, tracking, and evasion across the
attacking fleet.

Crit multiplier:

`attackerCritMult = 1 + (averageCrit / 200)`

Tracking edge:

`trackingEdge = clamp((attackerAverageTracking - defenderAverageEvasion) / 200,`
`                     -0.2,`
`                      0.2)`

`attackerTrackingMult = 1 + trackingEdge`

Final attacker power:

`attackerPower = attackerAttackBase`
`              * attackerCritMult`
`              * attackerTrackingMult`
`              * fleetAttackMultiplier`
`              * globalAttackMultiplier`

Important notes:
- Varkon attack and raid fleets use a huge `3.0x` fleet attack multiplier
- Veil War-path `Eclipse Ascension` gives `1.25x` fleet attack
- SPU fleet attack bonuses multiply here too

STEP 6: DEFENDER POWER
----------------------
The defender gets three major components:

1. unit attack after crit
2. unit defense after defense multiplier
3. flat base defense from buildings after defense multiplier

Unit attack:

`defenderAttack = sum(shipCount * defenderWeightedAttack)`

`defenderCritMult = 1 + (defenderAverageCrit / 200)`

`defenderAttackAfterCrit = defenderAttack * defenderCritMult`

Unit defense:

`defenderUnitDefenseRaw = sum(shipCount * defenderWeightedDefense)`

Then the base's effective defense multiplier applies:

`defenderUnitDefenseScaled = defenderUnitDefenseRaw * effectiveDefenseMultiplier`

Flat base defense:
- Ion Shield flat base defense depends on defender culture:
  - Astraean: `75 * level`
  - Varkon: `10 * level`
  - Veil: `50 * level`
- the shield's flat defense is scaled by current shield integrity

`defenderBaseDefenseScaled = baseDefenseFlat * effectiveDefenseMultiplier`

Global defense multiplier then applies:

`defenderUnitDefenseAfterGlobal = defenderUnitDefenseScaled * globalDefenseMultiplier`
`defenderBaseDefenseAfterGlobal = defenderBaseDefenseScaled * globalDefenseMultiplier`

Defender tracking then modifies the full defender bundle:

`defenderTrackingEdge = clamp((defenderAverageTracking - attackerAverageEvasion) / 200,`
`                             -0.2,`
`                              0.2)`

`defenderTrackingMult = 1 + defenderTrackingEdge`

Final defender power:

`defenderPower = (defenderAttackAfterCrit`
`               + defenderUnitDefenseAfterGlobal`
`               + defenderBaseDefenseAfterGlobal)`
`               * defenderTrackingMult`

ION SHIELD INTEGRITY AND DEFENSE
--------------------------------
Ion Shield integrity matters.

If the shield is damaged, only the shield's own contribution is reduced.
At full integrity:
- its culture-specific defense multiplier bonus counts in full
- its culture-specific flat defense bonus counts in full

At `50%` integrity:
- only half of those shield-specific contributions remain

ATTACKER DEFENSE MULTIPLIERS
----------------------------
Fleet defense bonuses do not increase attacker power.
Instead, after casualty fractions are computed, they reduce attacker losses.

Exact post-casualty rule:

`attackerLossFraction = attackerLossFraction / fleetDefenseMultiplier`

Examples:
- Veil War-path `Eclipse Ascension`: `1.25x` defense multiplier
- SPU fleet defense bonuses also improve survival this way

DOMINANCE CASUALTY SCALING
--------------------------
When one side is much stronger, the dominant side takes reduced incoming
casualties. This can help either the attacker or the defender.

Dominance is the larger of:
- power ratio
- ship-count ratio

The current formula is:

`dominantIncomingDamageScale = max(missionMinimum, 1 / dominance ^ exponent)`

Attack exponent: `0.48`
Raid exponent: `0.26`
Minimum incoming damage scale: `25%` on attacks, `55%` on raids

WORKED EXAMPLE A: FULL ATTACK INTO A SHIELDED BASE
--------------------------------------------------
Attacker:
- `10 Auric`
- `4 Quasar`
- `1 Lumen`

Defender:
- `12 Halo Automaton`
- `6 Quasar`
- Veil-owned `Ion Shield 10` at full integrity
- no extra tile defense
- no SPU or temporary buffs

1. Light/heavy shares

Attacker:
- light = `10`
- heavy = `5`

`attackerLightShare = 10 / 15 = 0.6667`
`attackerHeavyShare = 5 / 15 = 0.3333`

Defender:
- light = `12`
- heavy = `6`

`defenderLightShare = 12 / 18 = 0.6667`
`defenderHeavyShare = 6 / 18 = 0.3333`

2. Carrier aura

Attacker has `1` carrier:

`carrierBuff = 1.0 + (1 * 0.0001) = 1.0001`

3. Attacker weighted attack per hull

Auric:

`(65 * 0.6667 + 28 * 0.3333) * 1.0001`
`= (43.3355 + 9.3324) * 1.0001`
`= 52.6679 * 1.0001`
`= 52.6719`

Quasar:

`(55 * 0.6667 + 100 * 0.3333) * 1.0001`
`= (36.6685 + 33.3300) * 1.0001`
`= 69.9985 * 1.0001`
`= 70.0070`

Lumen does not buff itself:

`(15 * 0.6667 + 70 * 0.3333)`
`= 10.0005 + 23.3310`
`= 33.3333`

4. Attacker base attack

`10 * 52.6719 = 526.7190`
`4 * 70.0070 = 280.0280`
`1 * 33.3333 = 33.3333`

`attackerAttackBase = 526.7190 + 280.0280 + 33.3333`
`attackerAttackBase = 840.0803`

5. Attacker average crit

Auric crit contribution:

`14 * 1.0001 = 14.0014`

Quasar crit contribution:

`5 * 1.0001 = 5.0005`

Lumen crit:

`4`

Fleet average:

`(10 * 14.0014 + 4 * 5.0005 + 1 * 4) / 15`
`= (140.0140 + 20.0020 + 4.0000) / 15`
`= 164.0160 / 15`
`= 10.9344`

`attackerCritMult = 1 + (10.9344 / 200)`
`attackerCritMult = 1.054672`

6. Attacker average tracking

`(10 * 95.0095 + 4 * 70.0070 + 1 * 90) / 15`
`= (950.0950 + 280.0280 + 90.0000) / 15`
`= 1320.1230 / 15`
`= 88.0082`

7. Defender average evasion

`(12 * 28 + 6 * 8) / 18`
`= (336 + 48) / 18`
`= 384 / 18`
`= 21.3333`

8. Attacker tracking edge

`(88.0082 - 21.3333) / 200 = 0.3334`

The game clamps this to `0.2`.

So:

`attackerTrackingMult = 1.2`

9. Final attacker power

`attackerPower = 840.0803 * 1.054672 * 1.2`
`attackerPower = 1063.2115`

10. Defender attack

Halo Automaton weighted attack:

`80 * 0.6667 + 20 * 0.3333`
`= 53.3360 + 6.6660`
`= 60.0020`

Quasar weighted attack:

`55 * 0.6667 + 100 * 0.3333`
`= 36.6685 + 33.3300`
`= 69.9985`

Fleet total:

`12 * 60.0020 + 6 * 69.9985`
`= 720.0240 + 419.9910`
`= 1140.0150`

Rounded combat value:

`defenderAttack = 1140.0`

11. Defender crit

`(12 * 12 + 6 * 5) / 18`
`= (144 + 30) / 18`
`= 174 / 18`
`= 9.6667`

`defenderCritMult = 1 + (9.6667 / 200)`
`= 1.0483335`

`defenderAttackAfterCrit = 1140.0 * 1.0483335`
`= 1195.1002`

12. Defender unit defense

Halo Automaton weighted defense:

`26 * 0.6667 + 18 * 0.3333`
`= 17.3342 + 5.9994`
`= 23.3336`

Quasar weighted defense:

`110 * 0.6667 + 100 * 0.3333`
`= 73.3370 + 33.3300`
`= 106.6670`

Fleet total:

`12 * 23.3336 + 6 * 106.6670`
`= 280.0032 + 640.0020`
`= 920.0052`

Rounded combat value:

`defenderUnitDefenseRaw = 920.0`

13. Defender defense multiplier

For a Veil defender, `Ion Shield 10` gives:
- `+50%` defense multiplier
- `+500` flat base defense

So:

`effectiveDefenseMultiplier = 1.5`

Unit defense after multiplier:

`920.0 * 1.5 = 1380.0`

Flat base defense after multiplier:

`500 * 1.5 = 750.0`

14. Defender tracking edge

Defender average tracking:

`(12 * 115 + 6 * 70) / 18`
`= (1380 + 420) / 18`
`= 1800 / 18`
`= 100`

Attacker average evasion:

`(10 * 38.0038 + 4 * 8.0008 + 1 * 8) / 15`
`= (380.0380 + 32.0032 + 8.0000) / 15`
`= 420.0412 / 15`
`= 28.0027`

Tracking edge:

`(100 - 28.0027) / 200 = 0.3600`

This also clamps to `0.2`.

So:

`defenderTrackingMult = 1.2`

15. Final defender power

`defenderPower = (1195.1002 + 1380.0 + 750.0) * 1.2`
`defenderPower = 3325.1002 * 1.2`
`defenderPower = 3990.1202`

16. Raw loss fractions

`totalPower = 1063.2115 + 3990.1202 = 5053.3317`

`rawAttackerLossFraction = 3990.1202 / 5053.3317 = 0.7896`
`rawDefenderLossFraction = 1063.2115 / 5053.3317 = 0.2104`

Because this is a full `Attack`, one side must be wiped.
The larger loss fraction belongs to the attacker, so the game scales to:

`attackerLossFraction = 1.0`
`defenderLossFraction = 0.2104 / 0.7896 = 0.2665`

17. Rounded losses

Attacker:
- Auric losses: `round(10 * 1.0) = 10`
- Quasar losses: `round(4 * 1.0) = 4`
- Lumen losses: `round(1 * 1.0) = 1`

Defender:
- Halo losses: `round(12 * 0.2665) = 3`
- Quasar losses: `round(6 * 0.2665) = 2`

Result:
- attacker wiped
- defender survives with most of the base intact

TACTICAL LESSON FROM EXAMPLE A
------------------------------
Do not full-attack a defended shield base just because your fleet "looks large."
Weighted matchups, defense scaling, and tracking can turn a seemingly fair fight
into a clean disaster.

HOW RAID LOSSES DIFFER
----------------------
Raids start from the same raw loss fractions, then the game halves both sides:

`raidAttackerLoss = rawAttackerLoss / 2`
`raidDefenderLoss = rawDefenderLoss / 2`

Then overmatch rules may punish tiny or hopeless raid fleets.

If the defender massively outclasses the raid fleet:
- attacker losses are floored higher
- defender losses are capped lower

Using Example A as a raid instead of an attack:
- half-loss attacker fraction starts at `0.3948`
- half-loss defender fraction starts at `0.1052`
- overmatch is about `3.75`, so the attacker loss floor raises to `0.5576`
- defender loss stays `0.1052`

Rounded raid losses in that case:
- attacker loses about `6 Auric`, `2 Quasar`, and `1 Lumen`
- defender loses about `1 Halo` and `1 Quasar`

Raid lesson:
- raids are softer than attacks
- but badly mismatched raids still bleed hard

SMALL-FLEET ROUNDING
--------------------
Losses use:

`lostShips = round(originalCount * lossFraction)`

So tiny fleets behave sharply.
If you only send one or two ships, rounding often means "nothing happened" or
"everything died."

That is one reason probe attacks and tiny nuisance raids are unreliable.
