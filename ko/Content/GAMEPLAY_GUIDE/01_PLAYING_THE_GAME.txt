01) PLAYING THE GAME
--------------------

WHAT KIND OF STRATEGY GAME THIS IS
----------------------------------
Riftborne is a real-time simulation strategy game built around economy tempo,
fleet pressure, objective control, and map control.

Winning play links resource growth, expansion timing, travel speed, and fleet
pressure into control of the center, then converts that control through the
Origin Wormhole or faction Keystone Visions.

STANDARD NEW CAMPAIGN DEFAULTS
------------------------------
The standard campaign starts with these default settings:
- Base production: `1,000` per hour for each core resource
- Storage target at level 5: `5,000` per resource
- Storage target at level 20: `100,000` per resource
- Resource-building target at level 20: `10,000` per hour per resource
- Global fleet travel multiplier: `3.0x`
- Global attack multiplier: `1.0x`
- Global defense multiplier: `1.0x`

Your first base starts stronger than later colonies:
- `Silo 5`
- `Solar Cell 5`
- all four resources filled to current storage cap

That strong opening stock is intentional.
It lets you start building immediately instead of waiting for the first trickle
of income.

NO OFFLINE CATCH-UP
-------------------
Saved campaigns resume at the exact saved simulation time.
There is no automatic offline income burst when you continue a save.

Practical meaning:
- if you log out with queues half done, they are still half done when you return
- if you save while vulnerable, you load while vulnerable
- if you want Astra safety, shield safety, or queue depth, build it before you
  exit

THE CORE FLOW OF A MATCH
------------------------
Early game:
- reach `Central Spire 5` quickly enough to unlock the first strategic outpost
- turn your home base and first outpost into real resource throughput
- use infrastructure, raids, or both to lift income beyond the starting stock
- scout your local ring and decide whether you can expand greedily or must
  respect a nearby threat
- keep Astra positive while the first ship queues come online

Midgame:
- hit `Central Spire 20` on a base that can launch and defend new colonies
- push strong colonies beyond `Central Spire 20` when you need more strategic
  outposts or asteroid mining sites from the same origin
- turn outposts, colonies, and trade routes into a stronger logistics network
- split bases into roles instead of making every colony mediocre
- use raids and selective attacks to slow rivals, clear lanes, and take out
  exposed competitors without overcommitting

Late game:
- stop thinking only in terms of income and start thinking in terms of travel
  time, launch distance, objective pressure, and core control
- prepare a base or cluster that can actually hold central pressure
- contest `(0,0)` only when your economy can survive the retaliation
- clear and hold Keystone sites when your faction is ready to fund their Vision
  conversion

YOUR FIRST SESSION: A SAFE PLAYBOOK
-----------------------------------
1. Spend the full starting stock.
   A full warehouse that is doing nothing is wasted tempo.

2. Build Central Spire earlier than feels comfortable.
   Expansion is hard-gated by Spire milestones, so a late Spire means a late
   outpost, a late colony, and a late everything.

3. Get real resource throughput online immediately.
   Early game is about the first mining outpost plus infrastructure or raids
   that keep production climbing.

4. Keep Astra stable while ship training starts.
   Astra pays ship upkeep, fuels Solar scaling, and is still the fastest way to
   lose a stable empire if you ignore it.

5. Build the right Shipyards before you think you need them.
   Each hull family uses its own shipyard for training and travel tempo, so
   shipyards still improve offense, defense, logistics, and reaction time.

6. Scout before you commit.
   Many losing attacks start as "I assumed that base was weak."

7. Expand where the lane is defensible, not only where the tile is rich.
   A rich colony that you cannot reinforce is often just free loot for someone
   else.

8. Treat Keystone funding as a timing decision, not an automatic rush.
   A Keystone that starves your build tempo is weaker than one backed by a real
   economy and enough defense to hold it.

WHAT GOOD PROGRESSION LOOKS LIKE
--------------------------------
You are usually in a strong position when:
- Astra stays positive even while training ships
- at least one base has meaningful ship throughput
- at least one base is pushing toward outposts or colonies
- you can replace raid losses quickly
- you know which rival is the nearest real threat

You are usually in a fragile position when:
- Astra is near zero
- every base is trying to do every job
- you have no fast-response base
- you are funding objectives too early and military too late
- you are launching fleets without current intel

A USEFUL HOURLY PLANNING QUESTION
--------------------------------
Keep asking:
"What is my empire trying to become over the next 2 to 4 launch windows?"

Examples of solid planning answers:
- "A safer economic cluster that can afford a second colony."
- "A raid engine that punishes exposed Astra."
- "A fortress line that buys time for Keystone conversion."
- "A fast-response network that can reach the central ring first."

Answers that often signal drift:
- "I am upgrading whatever I can afford."
- "I am building every nice-looking structure equally."
- "I will attack when the queue finishes, even if the target changes."
