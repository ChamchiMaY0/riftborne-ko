10) ADVANCED STRATEGY AND COMMON MISTAKES
-----------------------------------------

THE BEST OPENINGS ARE DELIBERATE
--------------------------------
A useful opening question is:
"What role is this base becoming, and what timing am I trying to hit?"

That keeps early upgrades tied to expansion, raids, defense, or objective tempo
instead of short-term affordability alone.

THREE STRONG EARLY IDENTITIES
-----------------------------

1. OUTPOST MACRO OPEN
- early `Central Spire 5`
- first mining outpost online quickly
- storage and resource buildings that turn the outpost into lasting throughput
- stable Astra while ship queues start
- first expansion only when the lane is defendable

Best when:
- neighbors are far
- your opening tile quality is strong
- you want reliable Keystone or center access later

2. RAID TEMPO OPEN
- shipyards sooner
- fast, cargo-capable pressure fleets
- scouting before commitment
- economy built around replacing raid losses fast

Best when:
- nearby rivals are exposed
- you can reach them quickly
- you understand cargo math well enough to make raids pay

3. FORTRESS INTO CORE OPEN
- Ion Shield and Astra safety
- controlled expansion
- base specialization
- late but powerful objective and center timing

Best when:
- neighbors are aggressive
- your local space is worth holding
- you expect a long match

WHEN TO RAID AND WHEN TO ATTACK
-------------------------------
Raid when:
- the target is economically rich
- hidden stock is low enough that visible loot still matters
- you can carry home enough value
- the defender is strong enough that a full attack would be wasteful

Attack when:
- you can actually win the field battle
- siege can convert the win into structural damage
- removing the base matters more than stealing a shipment

Reversing those decisions usually wastes fleets and tempo.

HOW TO READ A TARGET
--------------------
Ask these questions before launch:
- What is the defender's light-heavy mix?
- Is there an Ion Shield?
- How much hidden stock is likely on the base?
- Can the defender reinforce before I arrive?
- If I win, do I have enough siege and survival left to matter?

If you do not know those answers, spy first.

WHY MANY "WON" EMPIRES STILL LOSE
---------------------------------
1. They expand to the cap before they can defend the cap.

2. They fund objective structures without the storage, income, or defense to
   feed them safely.

3. They launch attacks from slow bases and arrive too late.

4. They treat central control as a final step instead of a long setup.

5. They win ships but lose Astra.

CULTURE-SPECIFIC STRATEGY
-------------------------

ASTRAEAN
- play for efficiency, mixed fleets, and clean logistics
- respect the slower build pace
- great for flexible midgame transitions and long stable economies

VARKON
- your biggest advantage is decisive committed violence
- use `Conscription Service` for real wars, not random skirmishes; its
  activation window is short and its post-window cooldown is long
- build the economy that lets you pick the timing, then hit hard

VEIL
- think in launch bases, shard timing, and asymmetry
- your best turns often come from mobility, special upgrades, and tempo spikes
- do not waste the culture by playing like a fair, static line faction

COMMON MISTAKES
---------------
1. Starving Astra to build one more nice structure.

2. Sending siege ships on raids that only wanted cargo.

3. Ignoring shipyard travel speed and then wondering why every response is late.

4. Assuming one good raid empties a rich target through `Nano Storage`.

5. Building every structure on every colony.

6. Hitting `(0,0)` too early and waking endgame pressure without the economy to
   survive it.

7. Treating Noctmarks, SPUs, and directives as side systems.
   In long matches they become empire-shaping advantages.

THE SIMPLE WINNING CHECKLIST
----------------------------
You are usually in good shape when most of these are true:
- I can afford my current ship upkeep comfortably.
- At least one base trains ships quickly.
- At least one base is built to hold pressure.
- My next expansion has a reason, not just a free slot.
- I know whether I am pursuing Wormhole or Keystone victory.
- I know which rival I must slow before the core war.
